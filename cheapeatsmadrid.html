On a budget in Madrid - Cheap Eats

Takos al Pastor

Calle de la Abada, 2, 28013 Madrid, Spain

The line at Takos al Pastor is one worth waiting in, especially for the deal you’re getting. Each taco is €1. After waiting 30 minutes in line you end up ordering more than you originally intended, but leave saying you could have ordered even more because they are truly that good. I recommend the cochinita pibil taco and la gringa quesadilla. If you’re in the mood for a drink, make sure to order a margarita - it comes frozen with salt and Chile wrapped around the rim!

Mercados

When I walked through the doors of Mercado de San Miguel, I found my heaven. I had imagined the idea of a mercado before I went to Spain - farmers market style, but inside - however, I highly underestimated the food quality and sophisticated ambiance. The mercado experience was one of my favorites - trying smaller plates of different foods (empanadas, squid, croquettes, burrata, patatas bravas) and drooling over everything else you don’t buy.

Convenient Stores

One word. Carrefour. You could honestly grocery shop at this place and be satisfied with your food. The quality of the meats and cheeses is amazing. I’d always stop into a Carrefour to pick up a water and snack or before I headed to a sunset destination to grab some bread, cheese, ham, and wine.

Café de la Luz

Calle de la Puebla, 8, 28004 Madrid, Spain

Café de la Luz is a cozy getaway from the bustle of Gran Via. The menu offers breakfast, brunch, and a wide variety of coffee and teas. My pan con tomate y jamón de serrano (tomato bread with serrano ham) and green tea cost me less than €4.

Menu del día

Menu del día is the way to go to get your bang for your buck. Monday through Friday for lunch you can get fixed-priced meal, which includes a starter, main dish, dessert, and drink all for 10€ - 15€.
