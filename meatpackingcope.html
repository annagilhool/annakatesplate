The Meatpacking District in Copenhagen

Mother

Høkerboderne 9-15, 1712 København, Denmark

Mother is not your typical dive pizzeria. As you open the door with one hand, the other pushes back a red velvet curtain to reveal a dimly lit restaurant, wooden tables, and a pizza oven, which exudes the smell of wood-fired pizza dough. Known for their sourdough pizza, the menu has plenty of options for everyone. I highly recommend getting a reservation, no matter your party size. Our second night in Copenhagen we managed to stop in and put over name on a waitlist. The hostess directed us back to their bar area — a tiny room with four intimate tables. You might be standing here for a bit, but nonetheless is a fun way to pass the time.

Warpigs

Flæsketorvet 25-37, 1711 København, Denmark

Warpigs is as rowdy as the name sounds. If you love craft beer, BBQ, or metal music this is a “have-to-go” place. Trust me - I’m not a metal music typa girl, but the atmosphere of Warpigs is a party. Get in line to order your BBQ, watch the chef chop your pound of brisket before your eyes, grab a craft beer from the bar, walk your platter over to the endless row of picnic tables and find a spot to squeeze in with your friends. You’ll be more than happy devouring your Mac-n-Cheese jamming to Franz Ferdinand.

John's Hotdog Deli

Flæsketorvet 39, 1711 København, Denmark

When in Copenhagen…you have to try a hotdog. This isn’t your typical American hotdog with ketchup and mustard. The Danish hotdog has more condiments and next-level sausage flavors. At John’s you’ll choose your flavor and bring your dog over to the self-serve condiment cart to top it off.
