Best Coffee Shops in Salamanca

Waycup

Calle de Velázquez, 126, 28006 Madrid, Spain

January, February, and the first few weeks of March 2020 I had the opportunity to study abroad in Madrid at IE University. The university is located in Salamanca — an upscale district with designer stores and a plethora of restaurants. During breaks in my schedule I always (as in the barista knew me) headed to my favorite coffee shop, WayCup, seconds from my classes. I didn’t start drinking coffee until I had a cappuccino from here. But, even better than the coffee are the cookies! The baristas warm your cookie for you, so you get a delicious snack with your pick-me-up. My favorite flavors are the peanut butter and carrot cake. There is plenty seating for studying or just chatting with friends. Quiero cookies.

Religion Specialty Coffee

Calle de María de Molina, 24, 28006 Madrid, Spain

My other go-to coffee shop was Religion. It is much smaller (so I recommend going solo or with one other friend if you plan to dine in), but young and trendy. When you walk in you see a neon sign lighting up “Coffee is my God” and a pastry case displaying protein balls, bagels, and croissants. When I only had a 20 minutes in between classes I’d run in a get the salmon bagel. Highly recommend! If you are planning on studying here make sure it’s only for an hour — the wifi has a time limit.
